MiDar App
Project Objective
With a topic of my choice, I need to come up with a platform that solves a problem.

What I Did
UX design, UI design, Lo-fi & Hi-Fi Wireframes

Tools I Used
Figma, Miro, The Noun Project

UX Design

Understanding the problem

The first step of this project was to find a clear problem that needs to be solved. In this case, I am designing a self-care app but I needed to figure out for what purpose and for who am I designing for.

Conducting

User Interview

Before conducting a user interview, I narrowed down my target users. I decided to make the app for busy working people. I interviewed 6 people where I mainly focused on asking about their schedule and how they do self-care.

After the interview, I made an affinity map to categorize the important and similar points they mentioned. As you can see, all of them needed better time management.

Creating A

User Persona

Making a user persona based on the interviews really helped me point out what the problem was.. In the persona, I gathered that my interviewees had a hard time squeezing ing some alone time because they work non-stop.

Problem Statement

Now that it's clear what the problem is, I needed to narrow it down into a statement.

How Might We Statement

After narrowing down the problem into a statement, how might we's are helpful by thinking of different approaches to solve the problem. In my case, it was to insert some alone time in the user's busy schedule.

UI Design

Work & Self-Care

Now that my concept is clear, I came up with MiDar, which is a calendar and self-care app. To answer my question in the early stage which is "for what purpose and for who"? MiDar is an app for busy people who can sync their work calendar in the app and at the same time set “me-time” activities throughout the day.

Crazy 8 Sketches

For this particular project, I did the crazy 8 method where I draw one idea in a minute. I eventually decided to go for the calendar + choosing activity idea that is marked with a blue dot.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Before starting to come up with the final look for the app, it is important to plan the screen and flow. After creating the Lo-Fi wireframes, I conducted another user testing with my previous interviewees and got their feedback.

Hi-Fi Wireframes

From all the feedback from the user testing, I was able to edit what did not work out for them and improve it. Then, it was time to come up with the design system of the app.

Unhealthy day tracker

Having an “unhealthy” day in your calendar is an indication that you need to do some activities to make it a “healthy” day. The number on your calendar will be marked red if your schedule is full and you did not schedule a "me-time" activity.

Me-Time Activities

Once you click the unhealthy day, it will direct you to a list of self-care activites.

Edit activities

You can choose what time you want to do the "me-time" activity and it will be added to your schedule immediately. There is also a "health tracker" to keep track of your progress and motivate you to do more activities.

Back to Healthy

Once you schedule a me-time activity and you've done it, they day will go back to being a healthy day.

Raffles Student Portal
Project Objective
Re-design The Raffles Student Portal Experience

What I Did
UX design, UI design, Lo-fi & Hi-Fi Wireframes

Tools I Used
Figma, Miro

UX Design

Re-designing A Student Portal Experience

The goal of this project was to improve the raffles student's experience when using Raffle's student portal. Again, it is important to ask for what purpose am I re-designing this website?

Studying the

Original Student Portal

Because I am re-desiging an existing website, it is an important step to study the features. I took notes of everything that works and does not work.

Conducting A User Interview

For this user interview, I interviewed 4 students who goes to Raffles Design Institute and asked about their experience with the student portal. From there, I got a lot of insights how what they did not like and what kind of troubles they go through while browsing it.

After, I compiled all their thoughts and opinions into an affinity map.

Creating a

User Persona

The user persona is obviously a student from Raffles Design Institute. From what i've gathered from the affinity map, I narrowed down their pain points so I could focus on the main problem to solve.

Problem statement

Then, I narrowed down the problem into a statement to get a clearer idea.

UI Design

Responsive Portal

While keeping most of the features of the original portal, I added extra features to increase engagement in the portal. Even making it responsive which was not available in the original one.

Sketching A

User Flow

Because it is important for the users to navigate easily, it is important to get a clear view of the flow of the portal.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

I proceeded to make the lo-fi wireframes and tested it out with the users. They immediately liked the new features I added and said the flow was much better. They did have some suggestions here and there of the unnecessary features.

Hi-Fi Wireframes

I maintained the colors of Raffles and edited the wireframes with the suggestions of the users.

Easy Navigation

The main focus for this problem was to make it more convenient to navigate. To achieve that, I decided that a dashboard would be an easier navigation bar that the users can access immediately.

Showcasing

Portfolio

Another feature is a portfolio tab where students can see all the works they have done in school. This is to make it more convenient for them to check their works.

Student Tab

In the student tab and school, I added new features such as "Assignments" and "Internship Opportunities". These are just a few features I thought would be needed by every student. From feedback I got from my users, they did like the new addition.

Upgrading the

Original features

Of course, from studying the original website, there were some existing features that were not working well so I upgraded those to be more accessible.
