Korean Air
(Re-brand)

Service &

Excellence

I was tasked to choose an airline that needs a whole new branding. Korean Air, despite being known for its great service and quality, needed a re-brand especially since its logo is similar to the pepsi logo.

Logo Mark

The mark is made up of the silhoutte of a Magpie’s wings - which is Korea’s national bird and mixed with Korea’s traditional colors called Saekdong (multi-color).

The final logo consists of the Saekdong Magpie wings wrapped in a blue circle. The order and combination of the colors come from Korea’s historical beliefs and wisdom. Each color has a representation of the concept of the ‘five elements’.

Type & Color

Provicali is very simple yet still has a touch of playfulness. This is the typeface used for the logo type. It can also be used mainly for headings. The tracking for the headings should always be at 30.

Founders Grotesk is very pleasing to look at. It is a good contrast to the primary typeface. This is mainly used for body texts and is the typeface for the slogan

Each color is named after what they represent in the Korean culture

Logo Treatment

Here are how the logo should not be used

Proportions & Exclusion Zone

The proportions and position are fixed in it’s respective sizes. It must not be changed or adjusted in any way.

The exclusion zone is what separates the logo with other graphic elements. Nothing should exceed the lines.

The minimum size for the logo is 2cm.
